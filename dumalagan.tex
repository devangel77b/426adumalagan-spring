\begin{abstract}
Opinions in social environments are often influenced by their peers through interaction rather than personal decision-making. In a school setting, students frequently discuss a variety of social questions with friends, which can influence how their views ultimately shape up. With our project, we aim to mathematically model how opinions spread within a high school grade by using a voter model. Specifically, using a simulated voter model based on real student data, the structure of social networks and the presence of stubborn individuals will determine whether a consensus will be reached or the groups will remain divided under the question, ``Is it ever okay to lie?'' We made this question inherently ambiguous on purpose, since it will be more likely that opinions will shift through peer interaction.
\end{abstract}
